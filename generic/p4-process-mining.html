Project ID: Patient Journey Process Mining

Duration

1 month (2025)

Industry

Healthcare / Business Management

Dataset

1,190 appointments, 10 columns
Event log with sequential activity records

Collaboration

Team Work

Skillset

Celonis, ETL, Data Visualization, Dashboarding, Throughput Time, Process discovery, Variant analysis

Data Source

Internal operational systems of Saúde+ Health Center

Project Scope

Analyze the end-to-end patient appointment process at Saúde+ Health Center to identify inefficiencies, understand patient behavior, and quantify key performance indicators like wait times, satisfaction, and cost. Using process mining in Celonis, the team reconstructed the "As-Is" process from event logs, mapped all patient journey variants, and built an interactive dashboard to provide actionable insights for improving operational efficiency and patient experience.

Key Challenges

  • Constraints of the Celonis Academic account limited advanced data transformations and enrichment
  • Diagnosing the reason for low patient satisfaction without detailed motives for complaints

Technical Approach

Phase Techniques
Data Preparation: ETL Data integration in Celonis, deduplication of case tables, defining activity and case tables with keys and timestamps.
Feature Engineering limited due to academic account constraints. Focused on core process mining features: activity sequences, throughput time calculation.
Reporting/Dashboarding 8-pages. Exploration by case, process and variant; Business overview; Patient segmentation; Regional trends and Time Duration Analysis

Results

3.59 / 5

Average patient satisfaction score

9 minutes

Average throughput time

16% of cases

Complaint rate

Key Observations and Future Improvements

  • Implement more granular data logging (e.g., reasons for cancellations/complaints) to enable deeper analysis
  • Investigate the high "Return Another Day" rate and patient complaints to address core service issues, not just time metrics.
  • Patient satisfaction is low across all regions and segments, especially among insured patients and those aged 36-55, despite short recorded wait times.
